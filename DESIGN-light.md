# Design System Strategy: The Cognitive Canvas

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
The Creative North Star for this design system is **"The Cognitive Canvas."**

We are moving away from the cluttered, "utility-first" look of traditional project management tools. Instead, we are building an editorial-grade environment that feels like a premium workspace. The system treats the interface as a living document where AI doesn't just "perform tasks" but "illuminates insights."

To achieve this, we break the rigid, boxy template look. We utilize **intentional asymmetry**, where the sidebar holds massive tonal weight compared to an airy, expansive content area. We replace harsh lines with **tonal layering**, creating a sense of depth that feels architectural and intentional rather than clinical.

## 2. Colors & Surface Logic
The color palette is designed to create a clear mental model: The "Engine" (Navigation) vs. The "Work" (Canvas) vs. The "Intelligence" (AI).

### The "No-Line" Rule
**Prohibit 1px solid borders for sectioning.** Boundaries must be defined solely through background color shifts. For example, a `surface_container_low` sidebar sitting against a `surface` background creates a natural edge without the "visual noise" of a stroke.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as a series of physical layers. We use the `surface_container` tiers to define "priority of focus."
* **Base:** `surface` (#f7f9fb)
* **Secondary Workplane:** `surface_container_low` (#f2f4f6)
* **Active Focus Cards:** `surface_container_lowest` (#ffffff)
* **The "Engine" (Sidebar):** `secondary_fixed` (#dae2fd) or `on_secondary_fixed` (#131b2e) for high-contrast deep navy states.

### The Glass & Gradient Rule
For elements that exist "above" the workflow (like AI suggestions or command palettes), utilize **Glassmorphism**. Use `surface_container_lowest` at 80% opacity with a `backdrop-blur` of 20px.
To denote AI intelligence, apply a subtle linear gradient to primary actions: `primary` (#392cc1) to `primary_container` (#534ad9). This provides a "visual soul" that flat colors lack.

## 3. Typography: The Architectural Scale
We pair two sans-serifs to balance authoritative editorial style with functional precision.

* **Display & Headlines (Manrope):** This is our architectural voice. Use `display-lg` and `headline-lg` with generous letter-spacing (-0.02em) to create an expensive, high-end feel.
* **Body & Labels (Inter):** This is our functional voice. Use `body-md` for high-density data. Inter’s tall x-height ensures readability in complex project timelines.

**Hierarchy as Brand:** Use `title-lg` for project headers to command the page, but keep `label-sm` in `on_surface_variant` (#464555) for metadata to ensure it recedes when not in use.

## 4. Elevation & Depth
Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering** rather than traditional structural lines.

* **The Layering Principle:** Instead of shadows, stack surfaces. Place a `surface_container_lowest` card on a `surface_container_low` section. The subtle delta in hex values creates a sophisticated "lift."
* **Ambient Shadows:** When a floating state (like a Modal) is required, use a shadow with a blur of 40px and 4% opacity, tinted with the `on_surface` (#191c1e) value. This mimics natural light rather than digital "glow."
* **The Ghost Border Fallback:** If a border is required for accessibility, use the `outline_variant` token (#c7c4d8) at **20% opacity**. Never use 100% opaque borders.
* **Focus States:** For accessibility, focus states must use a 2px offset ring of `surface_tint` (#5148d7) to ensure high visibility without cluttering the resting UI.

## 5. Components

### Buttons
* **Primary (AI Action):** Uses the `primary` (#392cc1) fill with `on_primary` text. Apply the subtle "AI Gradient" here.
* **Secondary:** No fill. Use `outline` (#777587) for the label and a `Ghost Border`.
* **Tertiary:** Text-only using `primary`. Use for low-emphasis navigation.

### Input Fields
Avoid the "boxed" look. Use `surface_container_highest` (#e0e3e5) as a subtle background fill with a `sm` (0.25rem) corner radius. The label should use `label-md` and sit above the field, not inside it, to maintain an editorial layout.

### Cards & Lists
**Forbid the use of divider lines.**
* **Separation:** Use `xl` (1.5rem) vertical whitespace to separate list items.
* **Selection:** A selected item should shift its background to `primary_fixed` (#e3dfff) rather than gaining a border.

### The "Intelligence Spark" (Custom Component)
For AI-generated content, use a container with a `surface_container_lowest` background and a left-accented "glow" using a 4px wide vertical bar of `tertiary_fixed` (#acedff). This signals to the user that the content was generated by the system.

## 6. Do’s and Don’ts

### Do
* **Do** use `xl` (1.5rem) and `lg` (1rem) corner radii for large containers to soften the high-contrast professional look.
* **Do** lean into `surface_bright` for main content areas to keep the app feeling energetic and clean.
* **Do** use `tertiary` (#00505f) for "deep thought" or "data-heavy" AI states to distinguish them from standard primary actions.

### Don’t
* **Don't** use pure black (#000000). Use `on_surface` (#191c1e) to maintain a premium, ink-like depth.
* **Don't** use standard "drop shadows" on cards. Use tonal shifts first.
* **Don't** cram information. If a view feels crowded, increase the whitespace tokens before decreasing the font size. This system breathes.
